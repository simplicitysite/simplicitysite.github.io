Rescuing the PaaS with Simplicity

2013-07-18 SkillsMatter, London No one really wants a PaaS, not really.
What we really want are the proposed benefits of PaaS, namely:

Low Friction Development and Delivery to support the necessary Speed of Change

Which means all, or some, of :

  • Quick to deploy applications
  • Utilise different environments that your application can be deployed to that 'feel' the same. eg, local development/ production.
  • Quick and Automated Provisioning of resources
  • Scalability.
  • Manage scaling of an application easily, via an API.
  • Reduce the need to consider infrastructure, using some higher abstraction.
  • A high level API
In this talk, David Dawson and Russ Miles of Simplicity Itself will show how to design Cloud-friendly, simple components, services and applications that can deliver on the promises that have loosely been collated around the term PaaS, without the need for an actual PaaS... Register at Skills Matter...
